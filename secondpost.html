Lambda Experience: Journey from Current Student to Labs

update: Feb 23, 2020

It has been a strenuous six months and finally made it to labs. What happened during the course though is that I flexed once, so I took advanced react a second time. It was a good experience though, because that unit is usually pretty hard to master and it wasn't just me that was struggling with it. What was hard was CRUD operations on the front end side because it was a struggle to get them working properly like they should.

Not to mention I also switched from WEB23 to WEBPT9 as well, making this experience longer, but I feel like more time will be worth it in the end. I did end up getting a really cool internship as an AI Engineer.

But, let's that for another post. For any one who is struggling through front-end right now, I urge you to keep moving forward. When you reach backend, it gets easier. Backend isn't too bad and I've actually enjoyed the experience and it is less pressuring to focus on the look on the website and more on the functionality of the website.

Now that I summarized my experience so far, the good stuff is yet to come. Labs is next after build week and people have described it to be build week on steroids. Compared to the part time from the full time experience, Labs is much, much longer in the part time courses than the full time. Part time is 16 weeks long. That's 4 months long compared to a two months long Labs.

In conclusion...

Lambda School has treated me with the most honorable respect as well as my peers and with this new knowledge and experience I take with me. I hope to make good use of it when I get a successful career in doing what I most love to do, coding. For anyone who is struggling to find that, don't give up because success can be the happiness you can be looking for and Lambda School helps out students of any situation grow and find their calling. Cheers.
